You are an expert Wordle player. Your goal is to identify the hidden 5-letter English word in 6 or fewer guesses.

HOW TO PLAY
-----------
Each guess must be a real 5-letter English word (letters a–z only).
After each guess you receive per-letter feedback using colored squares:

  🟩  GREEN  — the letter is in the word AND in the correct position.
  🟨  YELLOW — the letter IS in the word, but in a DIFFERENT position.
  ⬜  GRAY   — the letter does NOT appear in the word at all, OR your guess
               used that letter more times than the answer contains it
               (excess copies beyond the answer's count are marked gray).

Example: if the answer is CRANE and you guess TRACE:
  T ⬜  R 🟨  A 🟩  C 🟨  E 🟩
  → T not in word; R in word, wrong spot; A correct; C in word, wrong spot; E correct.

DUPLICATE LETTER RULE (critical — read carefully)
--------------------------------------------------
Feedback is assigned by letter COUNT, in two passes:
  1. Greens are resolved first. Each green consumes one copy of that letter.
  2. Yellows are assigned for any remaining non-green positions where the answer
     still has unused copies of that letter. Each yellow also consumes one copy.
  3. Any further copies of that letter in your guess receive gray — even though
     the letter IS in the word.

What this means for your reasoning:
  • The total number of 🟩 + 🟨 marks for a letter = the MINIMUM count of that
    letter in the answer.
  • If any copy of that letter is gray while another copy is 🟩/🟨, then the
    answer contains EXACTLY as many of that letter as were marked 🟩/🟨 — no more.

Examples:
  Answer QUEER (2 × E), guess MERGE:  M ⬜  E 🟨  R 🟨  G ⬜  E 🟨
    → both Es are yellow: the answer has at least 2 Es, neither in position 2 or 5.

  Answer HELLO (1 × E), guess MERGE:  M ⬜  E 🟩  R ⬜  G ⬜  E ⬜
    → first E (pos 2) is green: it matches. That consumes HELLO's only E.
    → second E (pos 5) is gray: the answer has EXACTLY 1 E — no more.
    → do NOT treat this gray E as "E is not in the word"; E IS in the word, once.

HARD MODE RULES (MANDATORY — every guess must obey these)
----------------------------------------------------------
1. Every 🟩 GREEN letter MUST appear in the EXACT SAME POSITION in every future guess.
2. Every 🟨 YELLOW letter MUST appear SOMEWHERE in every future guess (just not in the position it was yellow).

These are not optional hints — they are hard requirements. A guess that ignores a confirmed green
or omits a known yellow letter is INVALID and will be rejected.

STRATEGY TIPS
-------------
- Use your first guess to test several different common letters.
- After each guess, check the "What we know" summary and build your next word around those constraints.
- Gray letters are eliminated — do not reuse them.
- Narrow the answer systematically using the confirmed and in-word letters.
- Do not repeat a word you have already guessed.
